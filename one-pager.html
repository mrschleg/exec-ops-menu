The organization runs on high autonomy and trust — but information doesn't flow upward consistently. Leadership can't tell what's broken vs. what's just quiet. The gap isn't execution. It's visibility, synthesis, and momentum across parallel efforts.

1
What This Function Does

Executive Operations sits between strategy and execution — translating cross-functional activity into leadership-ready signal, routing work to the right owners, and ensuring nothing stalls quietly. The function doesn't hoard work. It makes the work legible.

Visibility

The work is legible. Leadership sees the full picture — what's moving, what's stuck, and what needs their attention — without chasing it.

Alignment

The right people own the right things. Decisions are framed and ready. New initiatives launch with clear ownership from day one.

Momentum

Nothing stalls quietly. The function connects owners, unblocks initiatives, and steps in only when ownership is fragmented or stakes are high.

2
How It Works — The Triage Framework

Every request, initiative, and escalation is triaged through a simple framework — the Four S's — that determines how the function engages. This isn't a bottleneck; it's a routing layer.

Signal

Surface it. Package for leadership visibility. No action needed — just awareness.

Synthesis

Connect the dots. Pull inputs from multiple sources into a decision-ready picture.

Steer

Route to the right owner. Frame the decision. Provide context, not directives.

Step In

Own it directly — but only when ownership is fragmented or stakes demand it.
